# 💫 About Me:
I'm Suyash Omprakash Vishwakarma, an Electrical and Computer Engineering undergraduate at Viva Institute of Technology (2024–2028), based in Vasai, Maharashtra. I build things at the intersection of machine learning, data science, and emerging computing paradigms and I do it while still in my first year.<br>My work spans the full ML lifecycle. I've shipped 7 production-ready models across domains like medical classification, price prediction, and sales forecasting not just notebooks, but serialized, deployment-ready pipelines. I've built and deployed a live agentic AI application (CIVORA) for a freelance client using Flask, Docker, and REST APIs, serving real-time inference with sub-200ms latency. That's not coursework. That's production.<br>On the research side, I published a noise-aware quantum circuit layout optimizer for NISQ systems open source, experimentally validated against IBM's real backend noise models and presented two conference papers at NCRENB 2025, winning First Prize for one of them. I also independently investigated causal hallucination in ML models, a problem that sits at the frontier of trustworthy AI.<br>What makes me a bit unusual for my stage is breadth with depth. I've done data analytics internships, AI/ML engineering roles, and quantum computing research concurrently, part-time, while in my first year of a four-year degree. I don't wait for opportunities to be handed to me.<br>I'm currently looking for roles in ML engineering, data science, or AI research where I can contribute meaningfully from day one not as someone learning on the job, but as someone who already has.
